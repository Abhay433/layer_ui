GLOF (Glacial Lake Outburst Flood)

LizoAI predicts, simulates, and responds to catastrophic glacial lake outburst floods by fusing terrain intelligence, satellite monitoring, and real-time climate signals. Designed for high-mountain risk assessment, early warning, and downstream impact mitigation, the platform identifies vulnerable glacial lakes to safeguard infrastructure and lives.

Step-by-Step Methodology

Multi-Source Cryosphere & Terrain Data Ingestion

  • High-resolution DEMs (LiDAR, stereo satellite, UAV where available)
  • Multi-temporal optical & SAR satellite imagery
  • Glacier outlines, lake extents, and moraine boundaries
  • Climate data (temperature, precipitation, snowmelt indices)
  • Seismic and ground-motion feeds (optional)

Glacial Lake Detection & Evolution Tracking

  • AI-based lake extraction from optical and SAR imagery
  • Detection of lake expansion, new lake formation, and shoreline change
  • Ice-dammed vs moraine-dammed lake classification
  • Temporal growth rates and volumetric change estimation
  • Confidence scoring for lake stability indicators

Dam Stability & Failure Probability Modelling

  • Moraine and ice-dam geometry analysis from DEMs
  • Slope instability and seepage risk assessment
  • Temperature-driven ice weakening models
  • Seismic trigger sensitivity modelling
  • Probabilistic breach scenarios (partial to catastrophic failure)

Breach Hydrograph & Outburst Simulation

  • Breach growth parameterisation based on dam material
  • Time-varying discharge hydrographs
  • Peak flow and flood wave travel-time estimation
  • Ensemble simulations across uncertainty ranges
  • Rapid recalculation as upstream conditions change

Downstream Flood Routing & Inundation Modelling

  • High-resolution terrain-constrained flow routing
  • Channelised vs overbank flow separation
  • Velocity, depth, and arrival-time mapping
  • Sediment and debris flow potential estimation
  • Progressive energy dissipation modelling

Exposure, Impact & Risk Intelligence

  • Identification of exposed settlements, roads, bridges, and assets
  • Arrival-time buffers for evacuation planning
  • Flood depth and velocity hazard classification
  • Confidence-weighted impact zones
  • Scenario comparison across failure magnitudes

Decision-Ready Outputs & Reporting

  • GLOF hazard and inundation maps
  • Time-to-impact and peak-flow layers
  • Probabilistic risk envelopes
  • Export to GIS, emergency systems, and reporting tools
  • Full traceability of assumptions and data sources
