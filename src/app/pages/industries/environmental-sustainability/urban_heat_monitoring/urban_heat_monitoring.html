Urban Heat Island Monitoring

Workflow Overview

LizoAI monitors urbanization impacts by transforming multi-temporal satellite data into interpretable heat maps that visualize the impacts of urbanization. By tracking changes in land surface temperature and impervious surfaces, the platform provides clear, time-sequenced insights into the environmental footprint of growing cities.

Step-by-Step Methodology

Multi-Date Satellite Data Loading

  • Open-source thermal & optical data (MODIS LST, MODIS EVI, ESA Land Cover)
  • Select summer date range of interest (e.g., May 2004 vs. May 2018)

Preprocessing & Harmonisation

  • Unit conversion (Kelvin to Celsius) for thermal data
  • Resampling vegetation layers to match thermal resolution (e.g., 500m to 1km)
  • Urban and rural masking using ESA Land Cover (Class 190)

SUHI & Index Computation

  • Compute rural baseline temperature (Tmean)
  • Calculate SUHI intensity (Tpixel − Tmean / Gaussian fitting)
  • Derive vegetation difference (ΔEVI) for correlation analysis

Spatiotemporal Mapping

  • Identify urban hotspots with significant heat intensity
  • Tag areas as high heat/low vegetation zones
  • Visualise spatial heat distribution and correlations

Delivery & Interpretation

  • Export SUHI intensity maps with urban boundary overlays
  • Provide simple temporal comparison (side-by-side maps of different years)
  • Outputs are research-ready, interpretable heat maps
